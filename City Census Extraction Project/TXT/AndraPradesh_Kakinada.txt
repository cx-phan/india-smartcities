City: Kakinada

Kakinada is Andhra Pradesh’s fourth largest city, adjacent to the mouth of major regional river, the Godavari. The city has the state’s second
largest port, and the country’s first to be built as a PPP initiative. A similar mechanism is bringing a new shipyard to the city. The city primarily
exports seafood and fertilizers.

4. Political Profile: Leadership and Administrative Structure
Architecture of Governance
Structure of Elected and Executive Bodies. Give
indication of hierarchies

The city consists of two regions, connected by bridges. The southern part,
Jagannathapuram, is separated from the rest of the city by the Buckingham
Canal. The northern area of Kakinada is the more modern part of the city, with its
recent extension. The Municipal Corporation of Kakinada governs the city. The
Corporation is headed District Collector and Special Officer, an IAS officer.
Subordinate to him is the Municipal Commissioner who is supported by one
Additional Commissioner and one Deputy Commissioner. The Secretary is the
elected head who presides over Corporators. Kakinada Municipal Corporation is
divided into 50 wards.
The Municipal Corporation collects taxes (water, land, property etc.), manages
city solid waste, provides building permission and its evaluation, municipal
schools and health care system etc. It also builds roads, drains; provide water
connection to the households lying under its jurisdiction.
The Andhra Pradesh Housing Board (APHB), formed by merging the erstwhile
City Improvement Board and erstwhile Town Improvement Trust of Twin Cities is
engaged in providing housing accommodation to the needy citizens at an
